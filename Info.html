What is stress ?

Stress is the body's natural response to any demand, challenge, or change. It's how your mind and body react when you face pressure, uncertainty, or situations that feel overwhelming. When you experience stress, your brain releases hormones like adrenaline and cortisol — these prepare your body to deal with the situation (often called the “fight-or-flight” response). A small amount of stress can actually be helpful — it can motivate you to study, meet deadlines, or solve problems. But when stress becomes too frequent or long-lasting, it can affect both your mental and physical health, leading to issues like anxiety, fatigue, sleep problems, or difficulty concentrating

What causes stress?

Students

1. Academic Pressure: Exams, assignments, deadlines, grades.
2. Overloaded Schedule: Balancing studies, extra-curriculars, and part-time jobs.
3. Future Anxiety: Worry about career, college admissions, or job prospects.
4. Peer Pressure: Fitting in, social expectations, comparison with classmates.
5. Lack of Sleep: Late-night studying or poor sleep habits.
6. Family Expectations: Pressure to perform well academically.
7. Financial Concerns: Tuition fees, living expenses, or part-time work stress.
8. Self-Image & Confidence Issues: Body image, social media influence, self-doubt.

Employees

1. Workload Pressure: Deadlines, high expectations, long hours.
2. Job Insecurity: Fear of losing job or uncertain career growth.
3. Work-Life Imbalance: Difficulty managing personal life alongside work.
4. Conflict at Workplace: Issues with colleagues, managers, or clients.
5. Monotony & Lack of Growth: Feeling unchallenged or stuck in routine tasks.
6. Financial Stress: Salary concerns, loans, or bills.
7. Commuting Stress: Long or stressful travel to work.
8. Health Neglect: Lack of breaks, exercise, or healthy meals due to work.

Stress Levels and Symptoms

Low Stress

Mild restlessness, occasional fatigue.

Moderate Stress

Sleep issues, reduced focus, irritability.

High Stress

Anxiety, headaches, burnout, mood swings.

Symptoms of Stress

Physical

  • Fatigue
  • Headaches
  • Muscle tension
  • Digestive issues
  • Sleep problems

Cognitive

  • Constant worry
  • Trouble focusing
  • Forgetfulness
  • Racing thoughts
  • Poor judgment

Emotional

  • Anxiety
  • Irritability
  • Mood swings
  • Low self-esteem
  • Feeling overwhelmed

Behavioral

  • Procrastination
  • Changes in appetite
  • Increased substance use
  • Social withdrawal
  • Nervous habits
